Humidity

This graph shows that the main pattern that emerges is that humidity varies with latitude â€” the number of cities that have higher humidity values is much greater near the equator than in areas further north and south (towards the poles). The equatorial and tropical areas receive, on average, more insolation, resulting in a greater amount of evaporation and consequently a higher content of water vapor in the air. For this reason, cities in these areas are the wettest. Although Northern Hemisphere cities are more representative in the source dataset than Southern Hemisphere cities, it is summer in January in the latter, which means that humidity is even higher at that time of year. This analysis also shows that the lowest values of humidity are found near the pole in the Northern Hemisphere, especially in winter, where less insolation, cold temperatures and surface ice limit the amount of evaporation.
