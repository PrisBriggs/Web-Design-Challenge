Wind Speed

This plot shows that regions close to the equator are characterized by light winds, while mid-latitudes experience much faster winds, particularly in the Northern Hemisphere, where the highest wind speed in the dataset is around 50 degrees latitude. According to the referenced source, "stronger winds over the oceans are mainly the result of the “smoother” liquid water surface that creates less drag on overlying wind currents. Land surfaces are rougher as a result of their mountains, forests, and even buildings and factories. All of these topographic features of the land slow down winds significantly over continents". Since at latitudes around 40 degrees south there is basically sea water and not land, wind speeds become higher in this region, as shown in the graph.

Reference: Article 3: The Wind Resource (andlinger center for energy + the environment)
